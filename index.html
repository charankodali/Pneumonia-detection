Pneumonia Detection System

Pneumonia is a serious lung infection that can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi. It affects millions of people worldwide and can lead to severe complications or death if not treated properly. Our Pneumonia Detection System can help you to determine whether you have pneumonia in your lungs or not. One of the common methods for diagnosing pneumonia is using chest X-ray images, which can show the presence of fluid or inflammation in the lungs. However, interpreting X-ray images can be challenging and time-consuming for human experts, especially in resource-limited settings where there is a shortage of radiologists.

Here are six symptoms and potential causes that may indicate pneumonia

Chest pain when you breathe or cough

Fatigue

Cough, which may produce phiegm

fever, sweating and shaking chills

Shortness of breath

Nausea, Vomiting or Diarrhea

Prevention

Ensure you receive recommended vaccinations, such as the pneumonia vaccine (Pneumococcal vaccine) and the annual flu shot, to reduce the risk of pneumonia.

Practice regular handwashing with soap and water to prevent the spread of infections that can lead to pneumonia.

Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke, as smoking damages the lungs and increases susceptibility to respiratory infections.

Cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, and use tissues or your elbow to prevent the spread of respiratory droplets. Proper hygiene helps reduce transmission of pneumonia-causing germs.
